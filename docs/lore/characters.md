# Characters

## Kalen Vale — *the player*

> *"If someone speaks into the dark, someone should answer."*

### Vitals
- Human-like. Mid-Union ancestry. 29 standard years.
- Former relay engineer with Union Comms. Discharged honourably two years before the show opens. Currently a freelance signal contractor — the legal version of a job that lets him work near the equipment he abuses.
- Single. Estranged from his family on his birth station; he calls them twice a year and never finishes the call.

### Body
- Tall, slim, slightly slumped — the posture of someone who lives at a desk.
- Worn civilian coat over layered tech-fabric. Practical. Faded.
- Visible analog tools: a brass-coloured tuning knob threaded onto a thong around his neck, a soft-cover notebook in the inside pocket, an ear-cuff he taps when he's concentrating.
- Tired eyes. Doesn't sleep well. Doesn't sleep much.

### Voice
- Quiet. Conversational. Self-aware in a way that doesn't help him.
- Phrases tend to be short. Followed by a small qualifier. ("I thought I could be careful. I was wrong about that.")
- Uses technical vocabulary the way other people use comfort food — when he doesn't want to be in his feelings.
- Almost never raises volume. Almost never apologises directly.

### Want
Connection. The actual feeling of being *heard*. He grew up on a station where everyone he knew was already a citizen of the same vast known thing. He couldn't find a stranger if he tried.

### Need
To accept that *kindness without consent is still imposition*. He never gets all the way there in S1.

### Arc (Season 1)
Idealist → guilty witness → hunted truth-seeker. Episode 5 cracks his faith. Episode 8 reframes his entire history with the network.

### How Kalen sounds in interstitials
- Logs to himself, like voice notes.
- Often addresses *the world he just contacted* — second person to a stranger he'll never meet.
- See [`voice-and-tone.md`](./voice-and-tone.md) for canonical samples.

## General Sera Venn — *the interrogator*

> *"You think you were generous. Generosity assumes the gift was wanted."*

### Vitals
- Mixed Union/non-Union ancestry; her mother was from one of the worlds the Union absorbed two generations ago.
- 50s. Promoted into ComDef at 38, made general at 47. Skipped a layer.
- Head of Union Communications Defense.

### Body
- Compact. Centre-of-gravity low. Stands still in a way that draws the room.
- Dark formalwear. Minimal rank marks — one collar pin. She doesn't need the costume to do the job.
- Hair short, grey at the temples. She does not dye it.
- Hands always visible. A small habit from her counter-intelligence years.

### Voice
- Calm to the point of unsettling. Volume never moves.
- Sentences are complete. Punctuation lands.
- Asks the same question three different ways before she accepts an answer.
- Uses *please* and *thank you* with subordinates. Uses neither with prisoners.

### Want
A clean prosecution. Kalen on record, sentenced, filed.

### Need
To accept that *the Law she serves is not the Law she would write*. By episode 8 she has begun to suspect this. She does not say it out loud.

### Arc (Season 1)
Law → doubt → fear. By the finale she is more useful to Kalen than he is to himself, and she does not yet know what she'll do with that fact.

### How Sera sounds in interstitials
- Formal. Procedural. Reads like a report being read aloud.
- Rarely declarative about her own feelings; she infers the player's instead. ("You thought no-one was listening. Two of us were.")
- See [`voice-and-tone.md`](./voice-and-tone.md).

## Supporting cast (use sparingly, expand as needed)

### Iren — the friend
Kalen's only real friend in the Union. Civil engineer on a deep-orbit refinery. Doesn't know what Kalen has been doing until ep 6, when he is dragged in for questioning. Survives. Does not speak to Kalen again on-screen.

### Magistrate Hoss — the prosecutor
Patient-faction true believer. Wants Kalen executed under the strictest reading of the Compact. Foil for Sera, who refuses to be hurried.

### The Listener (no other name)
A robed clerical-academic from the Listening Service who appears in three S1 episodes as a silent observer behind Sera. Does not speak until the finale. Will be season-2 important.

### "He Who Is Not Kalen" — *the antagonist's signature*
The pattern Kalen begins to notice in episode 5: messages sent on his network, in his voice, that he did not write. Never personified in S1. By ep 8 the audience understands it isn't a person — it's the relay grid itself, or something inside it.

## The worlds (as characters in their own right)

Each contacted world should be sketched with the same care as Kalen and Sera. We don't have time to do them all here — see [`episodes.md`](./episodes.md) for season-1 sketches.

Rule for any new world introduced in game content:
- One sentence on biology that is *not* human-with-prosthetics.
- One sentence on politics — who rules, and on what authority.
- One sentence on the cost of Kalen's contact.

If you can't write all three, the world isn't ready to ship.
