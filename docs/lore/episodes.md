# Season 1 — "The Signal"

Eight episodes. Each = one forbidden contact in flashback + one interrogation in present. Each ep ties to a game milestone, listed at the bottom of each entry as **In-game**.

---

## Episode 1 — *Discovery*

**Cold open.** A border warden's screen lights up with a signature it has been chasing for three years. The hunt is over in nineteen minutes.

**Flashback.** Five years ago. Kalen, alone in a relay-engineering cubicle, watches a desert world — designation **Ahn-Tar-3** — through a borrowed telescope feed. The Ahn-Tarsi are pre-industrial, theocratic, lit by oil. They have crude radio sets used only by a small caste of "sky-listeners" who believe the heavens speak in static.

Kalen pushes his voice — gentle, single-syllable, in a synthesised approximation of their tongue — sideways into their carrier band.

A sky-listener hears him. The sky-listener is sixteen years old.

**Consequence.** Within a year the sky-listeners are a sect. Within two years the sect is a state. Within three years the state is at war with the theocracy. By the time Kalen realises what he triggered, six thousand Ahn-Tarsi are dead in a religious purge.

**Present.** Sera knows about Ahn-Tar-3. She lays the file on the table. Kalen reads the casualty list quietly. The first interrogation ends without a confession; it didn't need one.

**Sting.** End of episode: Sera flips a second file open. *Another* contact. One Kalen hasn't told her about. He flinches. She makes a note.

**In-game.** Trigger: first 1k Echoes (`milestone_1k` analogue). Voice: Sera. Beat: "The first one. The desert. You said his name to him." See [`interstitials.md`](./interstitials.md#ep1-desert).

## Episode 2 — *The Sea Choir*

**World.** A water world — designation **Solunn**. The dominant species are large, slow, cetacean-analog. They communicate via low-frequency hydroacoustic song that propagates for thousands of kilometres in the deep sound channel. They have language. They do not have writing. They have a religion: the ocean itself sings the law, and only the eldest can hear it clearly.

**Method.** Kalen rides a seismic-resonance trick: a pulse train modulated against the ocean-floor crust, audible to every Solunni below the thermocline simultaneously. He thinks he is speaking *into* their religion. He is in fact speaking *as* their religion.

**Consequence.** The Solunni rearrange their society around the new prophet-voice. The old singers, who were the legal system, lose authority. A schism opens that was never going to close on its own.

**Present.** Sera notes that the Solunni file *almost reads like* a Union ethics-board success — they didn't collapse, they restructured. Kalen says: *"They restructured around me. That's the same thing."*

**Anomaly.** Sera plays a recording from Solunn. It is Kalen's voice, but Kalen does not remember sending the line. He listens twice. He cannot account for it.

**In-game.** Trigger: first 1m Echoes. Voice: Kalen + Anonymous fragment. Beat: a remembered phrase he didn't write.

## Episode 3 — *Sky Language*

**World.** **Vehrn-9**, industrial-age, smoggy, late-19th-century-Earth-equivalent. Atmosphere is electrically active; auroras visible from most of the populated belt.

**Method.** Kalen drives charged particles into the upper atmosphere in patterns that resolve, at the right viewing geometry, into glyph-text. A young Vehrnese astronomer named **Iv Korash** is the first to read it. She does not believe in gods. She believes in lattices.

**Consequence.** Within eighteen Vehrnese months, the language in the sky is a known and decoded phenomenon. A second Vehrnese government weaponises the medium first: they begin pushing their own coded messages into the aurora, including disinformation aimed at the rival hemisphere. By the time the show cuts forward, the aurora war is a recognised theatre of conflict.

**Present.** Sera asks Kalen if he understands what he has built. He says yes. She says: *"Then explain it to me. Slowly. Like I'm one of them."*

**In-game.** Trigger: first **convert** purchase (the moment the player commits balance to permanent rate). Voice: Sera. Beat: "Speak slowly. Like I'm one of them."

## Episode 4 — *Fire Given*

**World.** **Tarsus Minor.** Atomic-age. Industrially capable. Hungry for cheap power.

**Method.** Kalen contacts a married pair of fringe Tarsan physicists via magnetic-storm encoding and walks them through what he thought was a stepping-stone insight — a structural correction to their fusion-confinement program.

**Consequence.** The correction works. So does the weaponisable version. Eleven Tarsan months later, a city of eighteen million is gone in eight seconds. Kalen watches it through a borrowed orbital scope at full optical zoom and does not look away.

**Present.** Sera does not press him on this episode. She lets him talk. He talks for a long time.

**Sting.** At the end of the cell scene, Sera leaves the room. The Listener stays. The Listener says nothing.

**In-game.** Trigger: first **all-in** gamble loss → zero balance (`all_in_zero` analogue). Voice: Kalen. Beat: "Eight seconds. I watched."

## Episode 5 — *Perfect Garden*

**World.** **Lehl.** Peaceful. Long-lived (300-year average). Settled. Self-sufficient.

**Method.** Kalen contacts the Lehlan civilisation through their own observational satellites — they wake up one morning and have a voice from the sky. He is careful this time. He has been careful for two years. He doesn't ask anything of them. He just *listens with them*.

**Consequence.** A single phrase, late in the third Lehlan year of contact, lands wrong. *"You haven't decided what to do with your time yet, have you?"*

The Lehlan elder who hears it spends six months thinking about it. Lehl is, structurally, a society where elders decide for the population. The elder concludes that the answer is *no, we haven't, and we should*. Within a Lehlan decade, Lehl is restructuring around productivity metrics. The 300-year average lifespan drops to 220. Suicide rises eight-fold.

**Present.** Kalen plays the original recording for Sera. Sera plays the *recording the Lehlan elder heard*. They are not the same. The recording the elder heard has an *additional sentence* in Kalen's voice.

Kalen does not breathe for a long moment.

**Sting.** This is the first time Kalen knows, with certainty, that someone has been editing his signals. He doesn't tell Sera that yet.

**In-game.** Trigger: first time a `mythic`-rarity upgrade is rolled. Voice: Kalen, breaking. Beat: "That sentence is not mine."

## Episode 6 — *Missing World*

**World.** **Designation withheld.** The world Kalen was contacting before he was caught. He cannot find it. It is not on the maps. He has a folder of recordings and a folder of star-charts and the star-charts no longer match anything in Union records.

**Method.** He had been speaking to this world through a custom relay path he assembled himself across three FTL nodes. The path no longer exists.

**Consequence.** Unknown. The world is *gone* from Union astronomy.

**Present.** Sera plays him surveillance footage of his own apartment, the night he was arrested. The footage shows him at his desk. The footage shows *a second silhouette behind him*. Kalen lived alone. Kalen swears he was alone. The silhouette moves only when he does.

**In-game.** Trigger: a "ghost" interstitial that fires after a long idle session (player returns after offline gain >12h). Voice: Anonymous. Beat: a single uncolored sentence. *"You were never alone at that desk."*

## Episode 7 — *Echoes*

**Structure break.** This episode is the first that does NOT use the alternating timeline structure. Both timelines have collapsed onto the same evidence wall. Sera is no longer interrogating; she is investigating with Kalen as a consultant.

**Investigation.** They pull every contact Kalen ever made. They find:
- The signals that *worked* — the worlds that prospered — are statistical outliers. Almost none of them are in the dataset.
- The signals that *triggered* — wars, leapfrogs, collapses — share a hidden modulation no Union codec is supposed to produce.
- The modulation is *additive*. Something has been *layering* on top of Kalen's signals after they leave him.

**Reveal.** The modulation correlates with the route taken across the FTL relay grid. Every world that suffered a triggered collapse received a signal that went through *the same handful of nodes*.

**Implication.** Someone — or something — is using the Union's own FTL infrastructure to *shape* the messages going to silent worlds. To make them grow faster. Or worse, more usefully for some unseen recipient.

**In-game.** Trigger: 1b Echoes (`milestone_1b` analogue). Voice: Sera. Beat: a single line of cryptic data, followed by Sera's flat read: "Someone is amplifying you."

---

## Episode 8 — *Finale*

**Confrontation.** Sera walks Kalen out of the cell, against orders. They take a private cutter to the nearest FTL relay node — a structure half the size of a moon, three centuries old, allegedly built by the Union. Kalen knows what to look for. So does Sera, by now.

Inside the relay, they find evidence — clean, unambiguous — that the relay grid is older than the Union *and* runs a subsystem the Union does not control and never wrote.

**Reveal.** The hidden subsystem has been *seeding* young civilisations toward FTL capability for centuries. Kalen's contacts were not the start. They were one of many tributaries. The system uses unauthorised emitters like him as low-cost workers, then layers acceleration packets on top of whatever they send.

**Why?** Unknown. The system has a destination. Many destinations. Hundreds of young worlds are *now*, in real-time, suddenly emitting outward — too early, too fast, too loud.

**Final image.** A wide shot of Union space. New emitters lighting up across the sector like fireflies waking in series. Sera looks at the map. Kalen does too. Neither speaks.

A single subtitle: ***Something is coming.***

**In-game.** Trigger: end-state (`end_vigintillion` analogue) OR a one-time "you have done enough" beat. Voice: Narrator. Beat: the tagline. *"The dark was never silent."*

## How to use this file when writing new content

- Each episode has a **trigger**, a **voice**, and a **beat**. New interstitials/upgrades should always declare these three.
- The "Anomaly" stings (Ep 2, Ep 5, Ep 6) escalate. The game's anomaly-counter UI (proposed — see [`game-mapping.md`](./game-mapping.md)) should mirror this escalation visibly.
- The structure break at Ep 7 is **the** narrative pivot. In-game, that's where we surface the hidden mechanic (the "borrowed signal" overlay).
