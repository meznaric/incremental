# Story Bible — Echoes Beyond the Stars

## One-line

A young Union comms hacker secretly speaks to worlds that aren't supposed to be spoken to — and slowly discovers someone else has been riding his signals.

## Tagline

> *The dark was never silent.*

Use this as a boot screen, an end-of-season interstitial, and the lockscreen of any in-world propaganda image.

## Genre & tone

Dark sci-fi drama. Mystery. Political thriller.

Serious. Grounded. Tense. Less idealistic than classic space shows. Mystery, ethics, unintended harm, slow dread. Wonder mixed with guilt.

Inspirational starting point: *Star Trek*. But every recognisable Trek term is replaced — no Federation, no Prime Directive, no Starfleet, no Vulcans/Klingons, no transporters, no phasers. We are explicitly avoiding IP overlap.

## What the show is *about*

Three questions, one per season.

| Season | Surface mystery | Real theme |
| --- | --- | --- |
| S1 | Who altered Kalen's signals? | *Is good intent enough?* |
| S2 | Why are worlds being accelerated? | *Who gets to decide when a people are ready?* |
| S3 | What threat makes the acceleration urgent? | *Are laws moral if suffering continues underneath them?* |

The show is **not** about humans-vs-aliens. It is about a stable, bureaucratic civilisation grappling with the question of when intervention becomes harm.

## The Union (one paragraph version)

A large post-scarcity alliance of dozens of worlds. Old. Stable. Bureaucratic. Not utopian. Its founding rule is the **Quiet Law**: no contact with worlds that have not yet left their own star. The Law is practical, not sacred — history has shown that early contact more often causes collapse (religious war, technological leapfrogging into weapons, cultural death, dependency, extinction) than flourishing. See [`world-rules.md`](./world-rules.md) for detail.

## The main characters (one-line)

- **Kalen Vale** — 29, human-like, former Union relay engineer. Believes isolation is cruel. Lonely, smart, sleepless. *The player is Kalen.*
- **General Sera Venn** — 50s, mixed ancestry, head of Union Communications Defense. Calm. Sharp. Severe. Interrogates Kalen across the season.

Full detail in [`characters.md`](./characters.md).

## Structure of Season 1

Every episode alternates two timelines:

### A — Present (the cell)
Kalen is in a secure interrogation station. Sera questions him. The walls are matte grey. There is no window. The light is too even. As the season progresses, anomalies surface: signals on Kalen's network *he didn't send*, signed in his style.

### B — Flashback (the contact)
Each episode = one forbidden first contact. Always answers three questions:
1. **How did he reach them?** (the technical method — radio leakage, magnetic storms, deep-ocean sound, aurora encoding, etc.)
2. **How did they react?** (cultural/political consequence)
3. **What was the fallout?** (the part Kalen didn't predict)

No "planet of the week" feel. Every world has real politics, real myth, real biology, real cost.

## Season 1 — "The Signal" — episode arcs

Short version. Full beats in [`episodes.md`](./episodes.md).

1. **Discovery** — Union uncovers Kalen. First case: a desert culture hears him on primitive radios; his voice triggers holy war. Hint: this was not his first world. Not even close.
2. **The Sea Choir** — ocean civilisation. He speaks via seismic resonance. They think the ocean itself is talking. Society reorganises around prophecy.
3. **Sky Language** — industrial-age world. He encodes speech into auroras. Scientists decode it. Governments weaponise it.
4. **Fire Given** — energy breakthrough becomes mass destruction. Kalen sees real damage. Faith cracks.
5. **Perfect Garden** — peaceful world. Should have gone well. Small doubt destroys paradise. *Hint:* signals contain edits Kalen never sent.
6. **Missing World** — a civilisation vanishes. Records corrupted. No ruins. No trace. Impossible. Someone watched Kalen.
7. **Echoes** — Kalen finds the pattern. Many worlds evolve too fast after contact. The signal network feels *alive*.
8. **Finale** — Kalen was never alone. An unknown system hidden in FTL relay infrastructure has been hijacking his signals to *accelerate* civilisations. Why is unknown. Final image: hundreds of young worlds suddenly reaching outward. Too early. Too fast. Something is coming.

## Three-season mystery engine

- **S1 — Who altered the signals?** → answered in the finale (unknown system in FTL infrastructure).
- **S2 — Why?** → answered: because something *requires* mature civilisations and is harvesting / shepherding them.
- **S3 — Threat?** → answered with the binary endgame:
  - keep the Quiet Law (and let what's coming arrive at silent worlds)
  - or break it open and warn everyone (with the cost of mass collapse already documented in S1)

## Themes (use these to score every script + interstitial)

- Good intent vs bad outcome
- Ethics of intervention
- Loneliness at scale
- Information freedom
- Colonial guilt
- "Are laws moral if suffering continues?"
- Who gets to decide when a people are *ready*?

If a line of dialogue or an interstitial doesn't push at least one of these, cut it.

## Worldbuilding non-negotiables

- Science feels plausible. No magic-tech fixes.
- Faster-than-light travel exists but it is costly, slow, and rare.
- Communication lag matters. Some contacts take years to round-trip.
- Alien cultures feel biologically different — not humans with rubber foreheads.
- Politics matter. The Union has factions, the Quiet Law has critics.
- Contact has real, durable consequences. No weekly reset button. Damage stays.

## Visual DNA (one paragraph)

Deep shadows. Cold stations. Huge silent space. Practical ships. Analog tools alive next to digital ones — knobs, jacks, paper logs sitting on top of holo-glass. Old radio aesthetics inside an advanced future. Mood: wonder buried under dread. Space is beautiful and deeply unsafe. Full prompt spec in [`image-style-guide.md`](./image-style-guide.md).
